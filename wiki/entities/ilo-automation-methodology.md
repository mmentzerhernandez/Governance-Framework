# ILO Task-Level Automation Exposure Methodology

**Type:** labor-market measurement methodology
**Origin:** ILO Research Department, Geneva
**Year:** Working Paper No. 87 (2023); WESO 2024 update
**Capability area:** [[ca6-labor-displacement-early-warning]] (CA6-BP1)

## What it is
A task-composition analysis that (1) decomposes each occupation into tasks via the ILO/O*NET Task
Content Database, (2) assigns each task an automation-probability score (0–1, updated for
generative AI), (3) aggregates to an **occupation-level automation exposure index**, and (4)
cross-tabulates with national-survey employment shares to produce sector-level displacement
profiles. The 2023 paper produced the first developing-country-specific estimates.

## Documented outcomes
- 24% of Sub-Saharan African jobs have high automation exposure (clerical, admin, routine
  agri-processing).
- 68% of high-risk jobs in low-income countries are held by **informal workers** with no retraining
  access.
- Applied by Ethiopia's Ministry of Labour (2023) to the textile/garment sector.

## Transferable mechanism
1. Download the ILO task-content database and automation scores (public).
2. Cross-tabulate with your national labor force survey (ILO offers technical assistance).
3. Profile the three highest-risk government sectors (back-office, agricultural extension,
   financial services).
4. Track the **Automation Exposure Index (AEI)** quarterly by sector and formality.
5. Trigger: an AEI rise >0.05 within a sector over 12 months prompts a labor-market-impact review.
