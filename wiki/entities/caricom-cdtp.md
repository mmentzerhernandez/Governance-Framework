# CARICOM — Small-Island-State Regional Digital Cooperation

**Type:** regional pooled-infrastructure project
**Origin:** CARICOM Secretariat + Caribbean Telecommunications Union (CTU); World Bank financing
**Year:** CDTP approved 2021, operational 2022–2027 (6 participating nations)
**Capability area:** [[ca7-regional-infrastructure]] (CA7-BP3)

## What it is
A World Bank-financed ($50m) regional framework for **pooled digital public infrastructure** across
small island developing states. Governance: CARICOM Secretariat (coordination), CTU (technical
standards), national Digital Transformation Units, and a ministerial **Regional Digital Council**
deciding by qualified majority (3/4). Its most replicable feature is **shared procurement** — a
single regional tender for common components, achieving scale individual small states cannot.

## Documented outcomes
- Regional tender achieved 28% cost reduction vs individual cloud-service negotiations.
- A shared open-source e-government platform across 4 states cut per-country cost ~$3m → ~$800k.
- The qualified-majority Council resolved 3 standards disputes without external arbitration.

## Transferable mechanism — governance architecture for small-state pooling
| Layer | CARICOM model | Transferable to |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic oversight | Heads-of-Government mandate | Any regional summit decision |
| Ministerial decisions | Digital Council (3/4 majority) | Simple majority for lower-stakes items |
| Technical body | CTU (existing regulator) | Existing regional standards body |
| Procurement | Secretariat regional tenders | Secretariat or designated NIE |
| Dispute resolution | Caribbean Court of Justice | ICSID/UNCITRAL where no regional court |
