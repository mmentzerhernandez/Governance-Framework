# CBDR-RC in the Paris Agreement: Defending a Principle Over 30 Years

**Type:** multilateral principle-defense precedent
**Origin:** G77+China, African Group, AOSIS, LDC Group; UNFCCC
**Year:** inserted Rio 1992; preserved Paris 2015 (Art. 2.2)
**Capability area:** [[ca5-multilateral-negotiating-playbook]] (CA5-BP3)

## What it is
**Common But Differentiated Responsibilities and Respective Capabilities**, a developing-country
principle inserted in 1992 and successfully defended across 23 years despite pressure to remove it.
Defense mechanisms: (1) **principle bundling** (CBDR-RC linked to finance obligations, so weakening
one weakens the other); (2) the **NDC architecture** (nationally-determined, not internationally
prescribed contributions, preserving policy space); (3) **capacity-building investment** (CBIT,
funding negotiator teams); (4) **parallel technical forums** (placing experts in IPCC WGIII to
author the scientific baseline).

## Documented outcomes
- CBDR-RC preserved in Paris; developing countries set their own NDC ambition.
- 135 developing countries submitted enhanced NDCs by 2023: broad participation without coercion.

## Transferable mechanism (AI governance)
- Promote an **"NDC for AI"** concept: nationally-determined AI strategies with international
  support but no prescribed technology mandates.
- Advocate AI capacity-building finance modelled on CBIT.
- Place Global South experts in standards bodies (ISO/IEC JTC1/SC42, ITU-T FG-AI): authorship
  confers negotiating authority.
