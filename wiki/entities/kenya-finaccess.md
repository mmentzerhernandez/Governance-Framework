# Kenya FinAccess Survey: Informal-Sector AI Exclusion Monitoring

**Type:** national financial-inclusion survey (AI-exclusion model)
**Origin:** Central Bank of Kenya + KNBS + FSD Kenya
**Year:** FinAccess 2021 (AI module); FSD Africa 8-country extension 2023
**Capability area:** [[ca6-labor-displacement-early-warning]] (CA6-BP3)

## What it is
The most sophisticated financial-inclusion instrument in Sub-Saharan Africa. Its 2021 edition added
questions tracking **algorithm-driven credit decisions**: whether the applicant used a digital
platform; whether the decision was automatic with no human review; whether a refusal explanation
was given; whether appeal was possible. FSD Africa extended the method to 8 more African countries.

## Documented outcomes
- 61% of Kenyan mobile-credit borrowers received automated decisions with no human review; only
  12% of those rejected got an explanation or appeal.
- Automated rejection rates for informal-sector workers ran 2.4× higher than for formal workers
  across Ghana, Tanzania, Uganda, Nigeria, Senegal.
- Data fed Kenya's National Payment Strategy and the CBK Guidance Note on Digital Credit Providers
  (2022), a survey-to-regulation feedback loop.

## Transferable mechanism
Add **5–7 standardized questions** to any household/financial-inclusion survey: Did a machine
decide? Were you given a reason? Could you appeal? Did an AI advisory tool replace your government
extension officer? Has an in-person service been replaced by a digital-only process? Track annually.
