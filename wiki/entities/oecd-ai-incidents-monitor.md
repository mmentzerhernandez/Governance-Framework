# OECD AI Incidents Monitor & Classification Framework

**Type:** incident database + national reporting template
**Origin:** OECD.AI Policy Observatory
**Year:** 2023 (monitor + National Reporting Template)
**Capability area:** [[ca3-harm-reporting-redress]] (CA3-BP1)

## What it is
A continuously updated, searchable database of AI incidents using a **four-category
classification**, each mapped to OECD AI Principles:
1. **Accuracy failures** (error, hallucination, misidentification)
2. **Safety failures** (physical harm, infrastructure disruption)
3. **Rights violations** (discrimination, privacy breach, wrongful benefit denial)
4. **Security failures** (adversarial attacks, data poisoning)

The OECD also publishes a **National AI Incident Reporting Template** that a government can stand
up with only a designated email, an intake form, and the four-category classification process.

## Documented outcomes
- 1,000+ incidents catalogued by end-2023; 30% involve government/public-sector AI. [~UNCITED~]
- **Costa Rica** was the first Latin American adopter (2023), designating its science ministry. [~UNCITED~]
- Template referenced in the EU AI Act's Article 73 serious-incident provisions: the de facto
  international standard. Japan and South Korea adopted the classification. [~UNCITED~]

## Transferable mechanism
1. Designate an existing body as national focal point (data authority, ICT consumer division, or
   ombudsman).
2. Publish a complaints form / email and reference the four-category taxonomy in communications.
3. Report annually to the OECD AI Monitor using the submission template.
