# Digital Public Goods Standard (DPGS) & Registry

**Type:** open-source vetting standard + registry
**Origin:** Digital Public Goods Alliance (DPGA) — UNICEF, Norway, Germany
**Year:** Standard 2020; AI-specific guidance 2023
**Capability area:** [[ca4-development-finance-lock-in]] (CA4-BP2)

## What it is
Nine criteria a software/data/AI-model/content/standard must meet to qualify as a **Digital Public
Good**: open-source licence; SDG relevance; no significant IP barriers; platform independence; data
privacy & security; standards compliance; no digital-divide exacerbation; documentation
completeness; privacy-protecting best practice. The **DPGA Registry** vets and lists DPGs — a
pre-vetted catalogue development-finance projects can require.

## Documented outcomes
- 195 DPGs registered by 2024 across health, education, climate, financial inclusion, digital ID.
- UNICEF country offices in 50+ countries use it as a procurement filter; Norad and GIZ use
  registry status as a preference criterion; World Bank references it as evidence of reduced
  lock-in risk.

## Transferable mechanism
- Issue a digital procurement circular: all development-finance-funded digital/AI tools must
  either appear in the DPGA Registry **or** pass an equivalent open-standards assessment using the
  9-criteria checklist — an automatic filter against proprietary lock-in.
- DPGA provides free technical assistance to implementing governments.
