# Rwanda National AI Policy (2023) — Executive-Authority Model

**Type:** national AI policy (ministerial, not legislative)
**Origin:** Rwanda Ministry of ICT & Innovation (MINICT) / Rwanda Information Society Authority (RISA)
**Year:** 2023
**Capability area:** [[ca2-minimum-viable-governance]] (CA2-BP4)

## What it is
A National AI Policy enacted by **ministerial document, not parliamentary legislation**. It (i)
assigns AI governance to the existing RISA; (ii) applies existing data-protection law (Law
058/2021) to AI; (iii) requires **AI Impact Assessments** from a template embedded in the policy
(Annex B); and (iv) routes public AI procurement through the existing Rwanda Public Procurement
Authority with AI specs added as a bid-document appendix. **No new institution was created** and
the full policy cycle took **under 12 months**.

## Documented outcomes
- Cited as the model for Sub-Saharan African AI governance by Smart Africa and AU strategy docs.
- Enabled a substantive Rwandan contribution to the Global Digital Compact process.
- RISA reviewed 8 government AI deployments (incl. automated land registry, health-insurance
  fraud detection).
- GARI ranking improved 62nd → 52nd (2022→2023), largest single-year jump in Sub-Saharan Africa.

## Transferable mechanism
- Adopt the **3-page AI Impact Assessment Template** (Annex B) by ministerial circular: purpose &
  legal basis, affected populations, risk level, human oversight, redress pathway, review schedule.
- Replicate the procurement addendum model (AI specs appended to existing bid templates).
