# UNESCO Readiness Assessment Methodology (RAM)

**Type:** framework / diagnostic tool
**Origin:** UNESCO (Paris HQ, CI/ICT Division)
**Year:** 2021 (methodology); 2023 (updated facilitation toolkit)
**Capability area:** [[ca1-readiness-assessment]] (CA1-BP1)

## What it is
A **government-led, facilitated self-assessment** structured around four pillars: (1) Legal &
Regulatory Framework; (2) Sociocultural Infrastructure; (3) Economic Capacity & Research; (4)
Technological & Digital Infrastructure. Ministry representatives score 5–8 sub-indicators per
pillar via a structured questionnaire, then validate them in a multi-stakeholder workshop. UNESCO
field offices provide a trained facilitator **at no cost**. Output: a scored heatmap + prioritized
action matrix that feeds a National AI Strategy.

## Documented outcomes
- Ghana's RAM (2022) directly shaped its **National AI Policy (2023)**.
- Senegal's RAM (2023) produced a 47-page readiness report used at Global Digital Compact
  consultations.
- Paraguay's RAM flagged data-infrastructure deficit, prompting an ICT-budget reallocation decree.
- **27 countries** completed RAM by end-2023; 15 more in progress by mid-2024. Low-income
  countries have finished in as little as 6 weeks with a 5–8-official core team.

## Transferable mechanism
1. Request UNESCO field-office facilitation (free) via the National Commission for UNESCO.
2. Convene a 5–8-person core team across ICT, Finance, Justice, and the data authority.
3. Complete the questionnaire over 2–3 sessions using the 2023 facilitation guide.
4. Run one multi-stakeholder validation workshop.
5. Publish the prioritized matrix as the evidence annex to the National AI Strategy.
