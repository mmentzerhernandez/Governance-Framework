# EU GAIA-X — Lessons Learned (Including Failures)

**Type:** federated cloud/data-ecosystem framework (cautionary model)
**Origin:** GAIA-X Association AISBL, Brussels (Germany/France founding)
**Year:** launched October 2020; Trust Framework v23.06 (2023)
**Capability area:** [[ca7-regional-infrastructure]] (CA7-BP4)

## What it is
A federated cloud-infrastructure and data-ecosystem framework for sovereign, interoperable European
cloud services with data-portability guarantees — technical specs for "federated data spaces,"
Bronze/Silver/Gold sovereignty labels, a 1,300+ member association, and a compliance Clearing House.
Its **documented difficulties are as instructive as its successes**.

## Documented outcomes & failures
- Adopted as reference architecture for 5 European industrial data spaces.
- But only ~200 services achieved label certification by 2024 — far below targets.
- Consensus association governance proved **too slow** (years, not weeks); large US cloud providers
  joined and diluted sovereignty requirements.
- The national adaptation (**France's SecNumCloud**) — stronger sovereignty, faster national
  decision-making — outperformed the pan-European initiative.

## Transferable mechanism — what works for the Global South
| GAIA-X element | Transferable? | Adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| Federated data spaces | Yes | Use a regional body as operator, not an association |
| Tiered labels | Yes | Simpler 2-tier (Compliant / Sovereign-Compliant) |
| Clearing House automation | Yes | Manual review first 2 years, then automate |
| Consensus governance | **No** | Qualified-majority voting with 30-day decision rule |
| Industry voting rights | **No** | Government-only voting; private sector advisory |
| National sovereignty option | Yes | SecNumCloud model — countries can exceed regional minimum |
