# Brazil LGPD + ANPD Automated-Decision-Making Guidance

**Type:** data-protection statute + regulatory authority guidance
**Origin:** Brazilian National Congress (LGPD); ANPD (Brazil's DPA); SERPRO (federal IT company)
**Year:** LGPD 2018/2020; ANPD Resolution 16 (2024); EBIA 2021/2024; SERPRO toolkit 2023
**Capability area:** [[ca2-minimum-viable-governance]] (CA2-BP3)

## What it is
**LGPD Article 20** gives any data subject the right to **human review of an automated decision**
affecting their interests. **ANPD Regulatory Resolution 16/2024** operationalizes this for
automated decision-making and profiling: controllers must document decision logic, provide
individual explanations on request, and run DPIAs for high-risk AI. Brazil's national AI strategy
(EBIA) uses Article 20 as the legal anchor for public-sector AI accountability; SERPRO's toolkit
converts LGPD obligations into operational procurement/deployment checklists.

## Documented outcomes
- ANPD has processed **400+ complaints** under Article 20 since 2021 — a jurisprudence base.
- EBIA adopted as a model by Colombia (2022) and informed Argentina (2023).
- SERPRO toolkit used by 12 federal ministries; shared with Uruguay and Ecuador via MERCOSUR.

## Transferable mechanism
- Countries with an existing data-protection law can issue a **DPA guidance note** applying
  automated-decision rights to government AI — administrative authority only, no new law.
- Draft a DPIA template modelled on ANPD Resolution 16.
- Request technical cooperation from ANPD/SERPRO via South-South channels (e.g. CPLP).
