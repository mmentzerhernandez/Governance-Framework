# Nagoya Protocol: Inserting Binding Benefit-Sharing into a Global Agreement

**Type:** multilateral negotiating precedent
**Origin:** Like-Minded Megadiverse Countries (Brazil, China, Colombia, India, Indonesia, Kenya,
Mexico, South Africa + others) + African Group; CBD COP10
**Year:** adopted October 2010; in force October 2014 (138 parties by 2024)
**Capability area:** [[ca5-multilateral-negotiating-playbook]] (CA5-BP2)

## What it is
The first **legally binding** benefit-sharing instrument for genetic resources. Four tactics made
it binding: (1) **domestic criminalization as leverage** (Brazil/India/Peru biodiscovery laws
created facts on the ground); (2) **documented harm evidence** (biopiracy case files with
quantified loss); (3) **stepping-stone sequencing** (accept the voluntary 2002 Bonn Guidelines,
then return 8 years later with evidence of their failure); (4) **intersessional coalition
maintenance** (16 LMMC coordination meetings, 2002–2010).

## Documented outcomes
- Reversed 20 years of voluntary-only status; ABS clearinghouse established (130+ national focal
  points). [~UNCITED~]
- Brazil's domestic biopiracy law generated R$400m for its National Biodiversity Fund by 2022. [~UNCITED~]

## Transferable mechanism (AI governance)
| Nagoya tactic | AI application |
|---|---|
| Domestic law as leverage | Enact **training-data sovereignty law** (dataset disclosure) before treaty talks |
| Documented harm evidence | National study quantifying AI-driven exclusion (credit denial, displacement) |

| Intersessional maintenance | Stand up a **G77+China AI Coordination Group** with a small secretariat |
