# CA2 — Minimum-Viable Domestic AI Governance

Capability-area hub. The core claim: **no new AI legislation is necessary** to operationalize
meaningful governance — existing procurement rules, data-protection statutes, and executive
circulars suffice. See [[zero-new-legislation-pathway]].

## The five exemplars
- [[singapore-ai-verify]] — voluntary framework + open-source testing toolkit (MAIGF / AI Verify).
- [[canada-directive-adm]] — the most replicated instrument: the AIA, by ministerial circular.
- [[brazil-lgpd-anpd]] — existing data-protection law applied to automated decisions via DPA guidance.
- [[rwanda-ai-policy]] — full policy cycle by ministerial decree in under 12 months, no new body.
- [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] — a Model National Policy Template adoptable by decree in 30–60 days.

## Package components drawn from CA2
The [[procurement-lever]] (Canada AIA) and the [[regulatory-lever]] (Brazil ANPD) both originate here,
forming the heart of the **Anchor** phase.
