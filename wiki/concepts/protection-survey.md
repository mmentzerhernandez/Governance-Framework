# The Protection Survey

> **Package component #6.** Add 5–7 algorithmic-impact questions to the next scheduled national
> household or economic survey to monitor localized marketplace anomalies and workforce-displacement
> impacts over time.

## What it is
A monitoring instrument that embeds a small set of standardized questions — modelled on
[[kenya-finaccess]] — into an existing national survey, paired with the [[ilo-automation-methodology]]
Automation Exposure Index for sector-level displacement tracking. Together they form an
early-warning system for AI-driven exclusion and labor displacement.

## Why no new law is needed
It is a **survey module added to an instrument that already runs** (LSMS/DHS/LFS or a financial-
inclusion survey). The module questions and technical assistance are free from the ILO/World Bank.

## Transferable mechanism (steps)
1. Add 5–7 questions to the next scheduled survey: Did a machine decide? Were you given a reason?
   Could you appeal? Did an AI tool replace your extension officer? Was an in-person service replaced
   by a digital-only process?
2. Embed the World Bank STEP task-content module to capture informal-sector exposure
   (see [[world-bank-step]]).
3. Cross-tabulate with the ILO AEI for the three highest-risk government sectors.
4. Track year-on-year: a shift without improving explanation/appeal access signals governance
   deterioration. AEI rise >0.05 in a sector over 12 months triggers a labor-market-impact review.

## Trigger / timing
**Monitor phase (ongoing).** Produces the domestic impact dossiers used in the Engage phase
([[ca5-multilateral-negotiating-playbook]]).
