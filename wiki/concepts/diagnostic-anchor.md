# The Diagnostic Anchor

> **Package component #1.** Complete the UNESCO RAM to secure an evidence-based roadmap and
> validate national strategic priorities within ~12 weeks.

## What it is
The diagnostic foundation of the [[overview]] sequence. A government runs the [[unesco-ram]], a free, UNESCO-facilitated, cross-ministerial self-assessment, to produce a scored national AI
readiness profile and a prioritized action matrix. Paired with extraction of the country's four
[[imf-ai-preparedness-index]] dimension scores, this gives a data-driven picture of strategic
deficiencies at near-zero cost.

## Why no new law is needed
It is a diagnostic, not a regulation, requested through the National Commission for UNESCO and run
by existing ministry staff. Nothing is legislated; the output simply feeds the National AI Strategy.

## Transferable mechanism (steps)
1. Request UNESCO field-office facilitation (free).
2. Convene a 5–8-person core team across ICT, Finance, Justice, and the data authority.
3. Complete the RAM questionnaire over 2–3 working sessions (2023 facilitation guide).
4. Run one multi-stakeholder validation workshop (civil society + private sector).
5. Extract IMF Regulation-&-Ethics sub-scores to triangulate weakest dimensions.
6. Publish the prioritized matrix as the evidence annex to the National AI Strategy.

## Trigger / timing
**Diagnose phase, Months 1–9.** Low-income countries have completed the RAM in as little as 6
weeks; the user's 12-week target is comfortable.
