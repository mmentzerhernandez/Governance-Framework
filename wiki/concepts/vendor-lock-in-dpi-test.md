# Vendor Lock-In & the DPI Open-Standards Test

The mechanism behind the [[infrastructure-safeguard]]. Derived from India's [[india-dpi-stack]], the
**DPI Test** is a four-criterion check applied to any development-financed digital/AI infrastructure
proposal before contract signature.

## The four criteria
| Criterion | Pro-DPI (low lock-in) | High lock-in risk |
|---|---|---|
| **API openness** | Open, published, government-owned | Proprietary, vendor-controlled |
| **Data portability** | Government owns data, can export | Vendor cloud, export fees/restrictions |
| **Interoperability** | Open standards (W3C, ISO, ITU) | Proprietary data formats |
| **Governance** | Statutory body, multi-stakeholder | Single vendor / bilateral donor condition |

**Any proposal failing 2+ criteria triggers a lock-in risk review before signature.**

## Reinforcing instruments
- [[undp-dpg-standard]]: require DPGA-registry status or an equivalent 9-criteria assessment.
- [[world-bank-procurement]]: data-portability + exit-planning clauses (migration + APIs in 90 days).
- [[green-climate-fund]]: conditionality clauses keeping technology choice with the borrower.
