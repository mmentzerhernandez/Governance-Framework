# AI Governance for Global Majority Governments: Research Report

**Type:** report
**Origin:** Deep Research Coordinator (MiniMax M2.1 Deep Research Agent + Exa Neural Search)
**Date:** 2026-05-29
**File:** `AI_Governance_Global_Majority_Research.md`

## Summary
A cited best-practice advisory framework answering one question: *what proven, implemented AI
governance mechanisms can a resource-constrained Global South government adopt today?* It covers
seven capability areas, each with 2–4 best-practice entries in a standard five-field format
(tool/methodology · producing org/country/year · documented outcomes · transferable mechanism ·
primary-source link). Its central claim: a credible national AI governance architecture can be
assembled from existing building blocks within 12–18 months **without passing any new AI law**.

## Key Claims
- The fastest governance pathway (12–18 months) needs **zero new legislation**: existing
  procurement rules, data-protection statutes, and executive circulars suffice. Rwanda, Brazil,
  and Singapore prove it. See [[zero-new-legislation-pathway]].
- The most-replicated accountability instrument is Canada's open-source [[canada-directive-adm]]
  (Algorithmic Impact Assessment), adoptable by ministerial circular.
- The best counter-lock-in architecture is India's [[india-dpi-stack]] (G20-endorsed, 2023).
- A coalition of <15 well-briefed developing countries can rewrite consensus multilateral text,   the [[ca5-multilateral-negotiating-playbook]] (Doha/Nagoya/CBDR/GDC).
- A **minimum-viable 18-month package** of six zero-new-law instruments is sufficient: see
  [[overview]] Section B.

## Key Data / Evidence
- IMF AI Preparedness Index (2024) covers 174 countries; EMDEs average 0.33 vs 0.73 for advanced
  economies, but the Regulation & Ethics gap is narrower (0.41 vs 0.54).
- 27 countries had completed [[unesco-ram]] assessments by end-2023.
- 70+ Canadian federal AI systems have undergone an AIA; the tool is MIT-licensed.
- UPI processed 12.2 billion transactions in March 2024; [[india-dpi-stack]] (MOSIP) deployed in
  16 countries.
- Kenya's 2021 [[kenya-finaccess]] survey: 61% of mobile-credit borrowers received automated
  decisions; only 12% of those rejected got an explanation or appeal.

## Implications
The wiki turns this report into a navigable action plan: a government follows the 5-phase
[[overview]] sequence and stands up the six-component package, drilling into each framework's
transferable mechanism. The report is most valuable as a **menu of pre-vetted, already-implemented
instruments** rather than a single prescriptive model.
